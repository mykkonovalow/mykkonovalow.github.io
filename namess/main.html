What is this?

Welcome to Naṁėṡs! It is an onomasticon of collapsible names.

"Onomasticon" simply means "collection of proper names". And a name is collapsible when it is made of two overlaping words. Naṁėṡs itself is a collapsible word. Same as Haġrove or Dreȧṁber.

The dots above (the diacritics) are added for the ease of reading.

Why does it exist?

I find it neat.

This begun in 2021, when I saw fantasy map on Pinterest that had a forest named "Hagrove".

"Intresting!" I thought. It felt like was looking not at a mere grove with some hags – but the Hag Grove. As if the two words were invented specifically to describe it. Sure, you could re-purpose its parts to describe a troll grove or a hag mire – but that is obviously an after thought, as the words don’t fit nicely together.

In the same vein, divine or prehistoric entities migh speak in rhyme. Not because they're pretentious wordsmiths but due to the sheer gravitas of their presence crumpling and plowing this “new thing”: the language.

What does it offer?

I parsed ~9.000.000 entries of Wiktionary in my search for collapsible names.

Here's the results:

  • A curated list of X cools names.
  • An interactive tool to build your own collapsible names around your roots.

You also can go on my Buy Me a Coffee page and grab an expanded lists (Y names) and a generational log (Z names) if you want to "mine the data" for some unseen gems.
